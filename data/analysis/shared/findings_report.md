## 5. Key Findings

1. **Inter Miami / Messi Effect**: The Messi signing in 2023 Q3 produced the single largest quarterly narrative spike in the dataset — 147 articles, PageRank of 0.071 (#1 in the league), a 2.7× volume increase from prior quarters. Positive sentiment remained elevated through 2024.

2. **Toronto FC Legacy Inertia**: Toronto ranks top-6 narratively every year despite being the worst team in the Eastern Conference from 2020–2024. Pure brand inertia from the 2017 treble era.

3. **Charlotte FC Expansion Overhype**: Most overhyped club per avg gap (-14). Narratively ranked 22nd on average while performing at 8th — expansion buzz never converted.

4. **LAFC Well-Calibrated**: One of the few clubs where narrative rank closely tracks performance. Avg gap of -3.7 despite being a top-4 club every year — slight overhype driven by LA market.

5. **Seattle Sounders Underrated**: 3 MLS Cups + CONCACAF title in this window, yet consistently outside the top 3 narratively. The league's most underrepresented dynasty.

6. **Aggregator Bias**: A single domain (themaneland.com) with 12.75 avg clubs/article was inflating CF Montreal's centrality by ~3-4 rank positions. Filtered from network construction.

7. **COVID Signal (2020)**: Article volume dropped sharply in Nov-Dec 2020 (31 and 42 articles vs 75-106 average). Network density thinned significantly in Q4 2020.

8. **CF Montreal — Missing Reddit Subreddit**: `r/cfmontreal` was not collected. CF Montreal's Reddit metrics in the press vs Reddit comparison reflect only incidental mentions from `r/MLS` and `r/ussoccer`, not dedicated fan discourse. Its press-side centrality and narrative gap figures (§ club profile above) are unaffected — those derive from the press pipeline only.

## 6. Methodological Notes

### Sentiment Analysis — Why VADER

VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner) was chosen over transformer-based models (e.g., RoBERTa, BERTweet) for three reasons specific to this corpus:

1. **Domain fit**: VADER was designed for short, headline-style social media text — exactly the register of both sports journalism headlines and Reddit post titles. It handles informal language, capitalization, punctuation emphasis, and negation without domain-specific fine-tuning.
2. **Reproducibility**: VADER is deterministic and requires no GPU. Any researcher can reproduce scores exactly from the raw parquet files with a single `pip install vaderSentiment`.
3. **Interpretability**: The compound score (−1 to +1) maps directly to human intuition. This is important for the press vs Reddit comparison, where reviewers need to reason about score gaps without treating the model as a black box.

**Limitation**: VADER can misclassify sports-specific idioms ("clinical finish", "killer instinct") as neutral that fine-tuned models might score as positive. Future work could validate a sample against a fine-tuned sports-sentiment model (e.g., [SportsXAST](https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.00001)) as a robustness check.

---

### Topic Modeling — Why LDA over BERTopic

Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was preferred over BERTopic for this study because:

1. **Corpus size**: At 7,236 press articles and 15,795 Reddit posts, the corpus is large enough for stable LDA topic distributions but small enough that BERTopic's UMAP dimensionality reduction can produce unstable clusters across runs without careful hyperparameter control.
2. **Interpretability for sports audiences**: LDA's bag-of-words keyword lists (e.g., "transfer, signing, loan, deal") are immediately legible to a sports communications audience. BERTopic produces semantically coherent clusters but the representative documents are harder to summarise in a table.
3. **Longitudinal stability**: We track topic proportions year-over-year. LDA's topic assignments are stable given a fixed random seed and vocabulary; BERTopic's UMAP step introduces stochasticity that can reorder topics between runs, making longitudinal comparison harder.

**Note**: Topic labels were assigned by matching top-10 keywords against a predefined label dictionary, with `ensure_unique_labels()` enforcing distinctness. The 7-topic solution was selected by minimising perplexity on a held-out 10% sample while maintaining interpretable keyword distributions.

---

### Lead/Lag Analysis — Correlation vs Granger Causality

The press vs Reddit lead/lag analysis uses **cross-correlation** (Pearson r at lags −3 to +3 months) rather than Granger causality tests. This is a deliberate scope decision:

- Cross-correlation identifies the lag at which two sentiment series are most aligned, which directly answers "does fan discourse lead press coverage?" at a descriptive level.
- Granger causality would make the stronger claim that one series *predicts* the other after controlling for autocorrelation. This requires stationarity testing (ADF) and sufficient per-club time series length. With 84 monthly observations split across 28 clubs (~7 months per club per window after filtering), most per-club series are too short for reliable Granger tests.
- The full-corpus series (aggregated across clubs) is long enough (84 months) for a Granger test. This is noted as a recommended extension for the journal submission version.

---

### Regression — LOYO Cross-Validation

Leave-One-Year-Out (LOYO) cross-validation was used instead of random k-fold because:

- Club performance has strong temporal autocorrelation (good teams stay good). Random splits leak future information into training folds, inflating R².
- LOYO simulates the real-world use case: train on all available history, predict the next season.
- Negative R² values in some folds indicate that the model for those held-out years performs worse than a simple mean predictor. This is documented as a limitation — the model captures league-wide trends but not year-specific shocks (e.g., Messi's 2023 arrival). It does not indicate a coding error.

---

### Entity Network — Press Only

Named entity co-occurrence networks (club–player/coach/executive) were constructed from press articles only. Reddit posts were not processed through spaCy NER for entity extraction because:

- Reddit post titles are frequently fragmentary and lack the syntactic structure (full sentences, context) that spaCy's `en_core_web_sm` model expects for reliable entity disambiguation.
- Reddit body text was not collected for most posts — only titles and metadata — making entity density too low for a meaningful network.
- The club co-occurrence network for Reddit (which entity is this club?) uses club name matching (regex aliases), which is robust to informal language.
